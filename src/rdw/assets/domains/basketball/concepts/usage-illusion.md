# Concept: Usage illusion

**Domain:** basketball  
**Slug:** usage-illusion  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-22

## Definition

Usage illusion is the mistake of treating high usage rate as proof of offensive value or portability, without accounting for efficiency, turnover cost, on-ball vs off-ball role, and whether usage is forced by roster construction.

## When to use

- Warning on high-usage low-efficiency players in rankings
- Explaining why a stat model may downgrade a "star" box score
- Team-fit notes when a player needs the ball to be effective

## When not to use

- For low-usage high-efficiency role players where usage is not the debate
- Without stating actual usage and efficiency numbers from packet

## Related terms

- on-ball creation
- heliocentric offense

## Useful phrases

- "The usage is real, but a lot of it is below-average efficiency on high volume."
- "He's productive in a narrow role; the illusion is assuming the usage scales."

## Common misuse

- Claiming a player is "overrated" only because usage is high, ignoring defensive value

## Evidence that supports the concept

- USG% paired with TS%, AST/TO, on/off with and without ball
- Role change when teammate returns

## Evidence that does not support the concept

- Narrative about "ball hog" without numbers

## Writing guidance

- Always cite usage and at least one efficiency or turnover metric from packet

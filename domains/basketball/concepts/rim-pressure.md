# Concept: Rim pressure

**Domain:** basketball  
**Slug:** rim-pressure  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-22

## Definition

Rim pressure is the degree to which an offensive player forces help at the basket — via drives, cuts, rolls, or gravity that collapses the defense — creating advantages for teammates even when the player does not finish at the rim himself.

## When to use

- Describing drivers, rollers, or off-ball cutters who bend the defense vertically
- Explaining why assist rates or corner three rates spike with a certain player on floor
- Contrasting finishers who score at rim vs players who create rim events for others

## When not to use

- As a synonym for "good at basketball" without mechanism
- For pure spot-up shooters with no vertical threat
- When sample is all jumpers and no tracking/drive data

## Related terms

- spacing gravity — horizontal pull; rim pressure is vertical
- roll gravity — specific case for bigs
- on-ball creation — often co-occurs but not identical

## Useful phrases

- "The value is in the help he draws, not the finishing percentage alone."
- "Rim pressure shows up in team corner-three rate when he's on."

## Common misuse

- Labeling high volume three-point shooters as rim pressure without drive/roll evidence

## Evidence that supports the concept

- Drive frequency, rim attempts, foul draw, short-roll gravity, help-and-kick assists
- On/off team rim attempt or paint touch differentials (with role noted)

## Evidence that does not support the concept

- High scoring average alone
- Highlight dunks without season-long drive profile

## Examples

### Good

"His rim pressure is real but narrow — he collapses drop coverage in pick-and-roll, which is why the corner three rate jumps when he's on, even though his own rim FG% is average."

### Bad

"He brings elite rim pressure because he's an elite scorer."

## Writing guidance

- Name the mechanism (PNR, cut, roll) and the observable team effect
- Pair with sample (season, on/off minutes if available)

# How to read the usage-and-true-shooting comparison

The NBA.com surface compares changes in usage rate and true-shooting percentage between seasons. It shows movement on named dimensions; it is not a complete player-value ranking.

The paired numbers are the point. Dillon Brooks is listed with a **9.6 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **2.7 percentage-point true-shooting increase**. Jaylen Brown is listed with a **6.4 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **3.4 percentage-point true-shooting increase**. Shaedon Sharpe is listed with a **6.6 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **1.6 percentage-point true-shooting decrease**.

Usage-rate change tracks the share of team possessions a player ends while on court. True-shooting change supplies the paired scoring-efficiency movement. Without that second dimension, a larger usage share can invite a usage-illusion read. With it, the surface is more informative—but it still does not explain role, lineup, defensive, or playoff context.

The comparison keeps the source's documented season window and sample filter. It does not establish total player value. Those broader conclusions require evidence that is not in this packet.

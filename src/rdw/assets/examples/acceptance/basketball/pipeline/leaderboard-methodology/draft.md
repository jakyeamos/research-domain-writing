# How to read the usage-and-true-shooting comparison

This NBA.com surface compares changes in usage rate and true-shooting percentage between seasons. It is a comparison of named dimensions, not a complete player-value ranking.

The paired numbers matter. Dillon Brooks is listed with a **9.6 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **2.7 percentage-point true-shooting increase**. Jaylen Brown is listed with a **6.4 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **3.4 percentage-point true-shooting increase**. Shaedon Sharpe is listed with a **6.6 percentage-point usage-rate increase** and a **1.6 percentage-point true-shooting decrease**.

Read the usage change as a change in the share of team possessions a player ends while on court. Read the true-shooting change as the paired scoring-efficiency movement. A larger usage share without the efficiency context can create a usage-illusion read; the paired TS% change gives the surface more information, but not a full role or lineup explanation.

The comparison uses the source's documented season window and sample filter. It does not establish defensive value, playoff impact, or total player value. Those questions require role and lineup context that is not loaded here.
